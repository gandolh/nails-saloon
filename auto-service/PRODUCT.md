# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Worried or routine-due BMW owners in Târgu-Jiu and the wider Gorj county. They
arrive on a phone, often mid-problem: a warning light is on, a service is
overdue, a noise won't go away, or they've been quoted an uncomfortable price at
an official dealer and want an independent second opinion. They are enthusiast-
aware (they know what "M", coding, and a proper diagnosis mean) but they are not
mechanics; they are deciding whether to trust this workshop with an expensive
car. Their job to be done: **find a competent, honest BMW specialist nearby and
make contact today** — with a model/year and a symptom, get a clear next step.

## Product Purpose

BavAuto Gorj is the single-page marketing site for an **independent family BMW
specialist** workshop in Târgu-Jiu. It is explicitly **not** a BMW dealer and
carries no roundel — it uses enthusiast colour language (a BMW-blue, the M
tri-color stripe used nominatively) under a standing independence/trademark
disclaimer.

Its job is to convert a visitor into **a phone call** — the primary, measured
outcome. A click-to-call is the #1 conversion; WhatsApp (send a photo of the
warning light + model) and the quote form are strong secondaries for visitors
who'd rather not talk. Success looks like: an owner lands, feels in the first
seconds that these are people who genuinely know and love BMWs, and taps "Sună
acum" (or WhatsApp). The site also carries Romanian legal compliance pages (GDPR,
cookie, terms, ANPC).

Romanian-language only. Static (Astro), fast, deployed to a sub-path on a VPS.

## Brand Personality

**Enthusiast, expert, family.** Petrolheads who happen to be your local family
shop. The voice is honest, confident, plain-spoken Romanian: clear devize
(estimates), respected deadlines, and a genuine passion for the cars, the kind of
people who light up talking about an N47 chain or a VANOS rattle. Enthusiast
energy is the lead now, but the expertise still shows through specificity (models,
coding, diagnosis) rather than empty carbon-fiber theatrics; the passion is real,
not costume. The family angle stays: it's why you trust them with the car. The M
motorsport heritage is worn proudly, never borrowed dishonestly (no roundel).
Emotional goal: a BMW owner thinks "finally, people who actually love these cars
as much as I do, and won't take me for a ride."

## Anti-references

- **Cold corporate dealership.** No glossy official-BMW-dealer gloss, no distant
  corporate tone, no stock-photo showroom. We are an independent family workshop,
  and the warmth and independence are the point.
- **Cheap roadside garage.** No clutter, clip-art, neon, all-caps "OFERTE", or
  low-trust visual noise. Clean, restrained, credible.
- **Generic SaaS landing.** No hero-metric template (giant number + label +
  gradient accent), no identical icon-card grids repeated endlessly, no gradient
  blobs, no buzzword copy. The auto-template AI look is the enemy.
- Motorsport energy is welcome, but it must read as genuine BMW-enthusiast
  passion, not generic aftermarket-tuner cliché (no flame decals, no fake
  carbon-fiber textures, no neon underglow). The M heritage is the reference, not
  Need-for-Speed.

## Design Principles

1. **The call is the product.** Every section earns its place by moving a worried
   owner closer to tapping "Sună acum". If a section doesn't build trust or
   reduce friction-to-contact, cut it.
2. **Trust is shown, not claimed.** Specifics over adjectives — real models,
   coding, RAR authorisation, named family, honest devize. Concrete proof beats
   "world-class service".
3. **Independent, not imitation.** Enthusiast colour language (blue + M stripe,
   used nominatively) carries the identity; never the roundel, never dealer
   gloss. The independence disclaimer is a feature, not fine print.
4. **Mechanical and motorsport-confident.** Small radii, firm buttons, precise
   dividers — engineering precision, not soft or playful. The M tri-color earns
   more room now: still disciplined, but the red and the stripe can carry energy
   (accents, highlights, ratings), not just a single hairline. Confident, not
   timid; the passion is allowed to show.
5. **Phone-first, friction-last.** Mobile is the primary surface (visitors arrive
   on a phone, often stressed). Click-to-call and WhatsApp must always be one tap
   away; the quote form is the fallback for the call-averse.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target **WCAG 2.2 AA**. Body text ≥4.5:1, large text ≥3:1 (the graphite-blue ink
and deepened brand/WhatsApp colours are already chosen to clear AA). Full
keyboard navigation with visible `:focus-visible` rings, semantic landmarks, and
a skip link. `prefers-reduced-motion` is honoured throughout (scroll-reveal and
mobile-menu animations degrade to instant). Reveal animations enhance an
already-visible default so content never ships blank. Romanian diacritics
rendered correctly; tap targets sized for stressed thumb use on mobile.
